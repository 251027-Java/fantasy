What is Fantasy Friend?

Fantasy Friend is your ultimate companion app for fantasy football. Our app provides you with the tools you need to take a deep dive into your fantasy football performances. We aim to help you separate your process from your results by providing key insights to make better decisions. Designed to be used alongside your favorite fantasy football platform.

Our Features Include:

Discover new leagues

See all there is within Sleeper!

The Sleeper API is a powerful tool for accessing and manipulating data within the Sleeper platform. We use it to programmatically access the data within the Sleeper platform, and automatically convert it into useful statistics for you to use.

Leagues are groups of users, usually friends, family, or coworkers, who compete against each other in a fantasy football league. Leagues are created and managed through the Sleeper platform, and are accessible through the API.

Search by username

Find what leagues others have joined

Simply enter a username and search for any leagues that user has joined.

Don't be worried about someone else accessing your Sleeper account though, all requests are made through the Sleeper API and are read-only, meaning none of your data can be modified.

Only view the leagues of users you want to see. Search for any user and view the leagues they have joined. Make a mistake? No problem, you can always remove a user from your list.

Calculate stats for your leagues

some description

View normal stats such as wins/losses, as well as more advanced stats such as All Play (AP), wins/losses, and more. Anything marked as "AP" is calculated based as if every user played against every other user in the league, every single week, not just their specific matchup.

We calculate proprietary luck metrics for each user, based on their actual performance relative to the performance their AP record would have predicted. This allows you to see how much luck was involved in matchups.

Additionally, for leagues with a median matchup, we calculate the luck based on how close a user's performance was to the median, with being slightly above (lucky) or below (unlucky) the median being considered to be a part of luck.
